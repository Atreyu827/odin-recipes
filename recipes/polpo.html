Santa Lucia Stewed Octopus

I love octopus, and in my opinion this iconic dish, polpo alla luciana or Stewed Octopus in the style of Santa Lucia, is one of the loveliest ways to prepare it.

You simmer the octopus whole in a typically Neopolitan soffritto of garlic, hot red pepper and olive oil until it exudes its juices, then you throw in some chopped cherry tomatoes, olives and capers and continue simmering until the mollusc is tender. A sprinkling of parsley at the end, and that's all ther is to it.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs octopus
  • 1 garlic bulb
  • 2 hot red pepper, peeled and chopped
  • 1 lbs cherry tomatoes, chopped
  • Capers
  • Olive oil
  • 1 cup white wine
  • Salt & Pepper

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add garlic.
  2. Add octopus, tomatoes, caper. Stir in wine, stock, season with salt and pepper.
  3. Bring to a simmer, cover and cook for 1 hour or until octopus is tender.
  4. Serve with hot crusty bread.
